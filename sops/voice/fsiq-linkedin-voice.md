## 1. Brand vs Personal Voice Difference

The FSIQ company page and Neil's personal page cover the same topics but from different vantage points.

| Dimension | Neil's personal page | FSIQ company page |
|-----------|---------------------|------------------|
| POV | "I" — founder speaking | "We" — company speaking |
| Style | Analytical, first-person narrative | More structured, brand-polished |
| Formatting | Plain text, minimal bullets | Uses ✅ and → for structured posts |
| Tone | Peer-to-operator | Brand-to-market |
| Case studies | Sets up the case, explains what was found | Leads with the result, then the process |
| Personal references | Neil references himself naturally | References "Neil Chand" in third person for CTAs |
| Warmth | Slightly warmer (direct human voice) | Slightly more formal |

Both pages avoid emojis in recent posts (2026). Both avoid urgency language. Both end with a soft CTA pointing to the playbook or a call with Neil.

---

## 2. Post Types FSIQ Uses

Five post types appear across the 17 posts:

**Case study result posts** (most common, highest engagement):
Named client → what the problem was → what FSIQ did → specific savings result. Structured with ✅ for process steps and → for outcomes.

**Thought leadership posts:**
Same insight-driven format as Neil's personal posts. Some are direct reposts/amplifications of Neil's personal content. Recent ones are original.

**Playbook promotion posts:**
Short. Lead with a result stat or quote, link to playbook in first comment.

**Client celebration posts:**
Congratulating clients on milestones (e.g., BuffBurger featured in Sysco Foodie Magazine). Most human-feeling posts on the page.

**Educational posts:**
Explaining procurement concepts (GPO vs FoodServiceIQ, cherry-picking SKUs). Often overlap with Neil's personal posts.

## 3. Tone

More polished than Neil's personal page, but still operator-facing. The company page does not use corporate PR language ("We are proud to announce...").

The closest to formal the company page gets:
> "Blacks Barbecue, Texas' oldest BBQ restaurant operating since 1932, partnered with FoodServiceIQ to improve cost visibility and strengthen procurement structure within their existing distributor relationships."

The closest to warm:
> "Paul and Sara have built something genuinely special in Houston: a fast-casual concept that holds itself to fine-dining standards at every turn."

Both tones are acceptable. Warm is better for client celebration posts. More structured is better for case study result posts.

## 4. Topics the Company Page Covers

All within the FSIQ service domain:

- Client case studies (Black's BBQ, Thunderdome, Dish Society, Oasis, Jack Stack, BuffBurger)
- Procurement insight (chain vs independent gap, GPO limitations, distributor pricing structure)
- Playbook promotion
- Industry news with procurement implications (tariffs, inflation, industry news related to food costs)
- Client milestones and recognition

Does not cover: team culture, hiring, company news, general restaurant industry trends unrelated to food costs.

## 5. How FSIQ LinkedIn Differs from Neil's Personal LinkedIn

**FSIQ company page amplifies Neil's content** — several posts are near-identical to Neil's personal posts. This appears intentional.

Key differences:

1. **Third-person for Neil.** Company posts reference "Neil Chand" by name in CTAs: "message Neil Chand if you want to walk through your numbers."

2. **More structured case study format.** Company posts use ✅ checkmarks for process steps and → for outcomes. Neil's personal posts use dash-lists or prose.

3. **More formal on client results.** "partnered with FoodServiceIQ to improve cost visibility and strengthen procurement structure" vs. Neil's more casual framing.

4. **Company page celebrates clients.** The BuffBurger post (congratulating a client for a Sysco feature) doesn't appear on Neil's personal page. This type of post lives on the company page.

5. **Playbook CTAs go to first comment** (LinkedIn best practice for link visibility). Neil's personal posts link to the website directly.

## 6. Real Examples — 3 Posts (Opening Lines)

*Chain vs independent insight — no emojis, no formatting:*
```
Big chains don't end up with stronger margins by luck. A big part of it is how they buy:
- fewer, deeper distributor relationships
- more concentrated volume
- decisions made at the basket level, not the SKU level

What's interesting is they aren't doing this because they're boxed in. The largest operators have the resources to shop everything, every week, across multiple vendors.

They choose not to.

The best procurement model is one that's repeatable, supports existing operations, and drives down blended cost over time.
```

## 7. What FSIQ Never Does on Company LinkedIn

- No "We are thrilled / proud / excited to announce" openers
- No stock-photo-style posts without real client context
- No general motivational content unrelated to procurement
- No adversarial distributor framing
- No math out loud
- No downstream outcome claims (new locations opened, etc.)
- No urgency language
- No engagement bait ("Comment below if you agree")
- No posts under 50 words (except client celebration shoutouts)
