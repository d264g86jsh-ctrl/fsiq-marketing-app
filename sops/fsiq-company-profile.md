## 3. Core Value Proposition

### The Problem FSIQ Solves
Independent restaurants consistently overpay on food costs compared to national chains like Olive Garden, Chick-fil-A, and Applebee's. The gap exists because:
- National chains have dedicated procurement teams and massive buying leverage
- Distributors use pricing complexity and hidden markups to extract margin from independents
- Most independent operators — even sophisticated ones with $10M–$20M+ in annual food spend — don't know the gap exists or how to close it

### How FSIQ Closes the Gap
**Mechanism 1 — Buying power:** FSIQ aggregates over $2B in purchasing power across its client base, unlocking national-chain-level pricing on existing SKUs.

**Mechanism 2 — Insider knowledge:** FSIQ's leadership team spent decades as COOs and Presidents of the largest food distributors in the country (including Sysco and PFG). They know exactly where distributors hide margin and how to recover it.

**The result:** 5–7% average annual reduction in food costs, with no changes to ingredients, distributors, or operations.

### The Model
- 100% performance-based — zero upfront cost
- FSIQ shares a small percentage of the savings they secure
- If they find no savings, the client pays nothing
- No disruption to existing vendor relationships

## 6. Target Audience

### Primary Qualifier
Independent restaurant operators spending **$1M+ per year on food** OR doing **$3M–$5M+ in annual revenue.**

Scripts use different thresholds depending on the ad set. The thresholds are:
- **Broad:** $1M+ food spend / $3M+ annual revenue (most common for cold traffic)
- **Mid-tier:** $5M–$50M annual revenue (Rob Tier ads, Exclusivity ads)
- **High-ticket:** $10M+ revenue / $2M+ food spend (VSL 5, High Ticket ad sets)

### Qualification Logic
These thresholds are **used in hooks as self-qualifying filters** — they pre-qualify viewers before they engage with the body. Scripts often open with: "If your restaurant does over $3M per year in revenue…" or "This only works if you spend over $1M per year on food."

### Who FSIQ Does NOT Work With
- Restaurants spending under $1M/year on food
- Restaurants buying almost exclusively through high-end specialty distributors
- Chains that already have dedicated internal procurement teams at national scale

**Scripts occasionally mention disqualifiers explicitly to build credibility** — e.g., "We can't help everyone" or "If you do under $3M this won't be a fit." This has tested well (Success Rate Ad).

### Job Titles in Audience
- Restaurant owners
- General managers
- Directors of operations
- Multi-unit operators / restaurant groups
- Directors of culinary / procurement (for larger groups)

## 10. Messaging Hierarchy

Use this order when constructing ad bodies — start with what resonates most on cold traffic:

1. **Savings outcome** — "5–7% annually, no changes to distributors or SKUs"
2. **Scale proof** — "2,000+ restaurants, $2B+ in buying power"
3. **Founder credibility** — "former COO/President of the largest food distributors"
4. **No-risk model** — "100% performance-based, zero upfront cost"
5. **No disruption** — "no changes to ingredients or suppliers"
6. **Specific case study** — MaryAnn's, Cincinnati, Spirits (use selectively)
