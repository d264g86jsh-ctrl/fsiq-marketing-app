## 1. Two Voices, Two Targets

This SOP governs two distinct LinkedIn presences:

| Target | Voice | POV | Tone |
|--------|-------|-----|------|
| `neil-personal` | Neil Chand — operator to operator | "I" / "we" | Analytical, direct, peer-level |
| `fsiq-company` | FoodServiceIQ brand | "We" / "Our" | Polished but not corporate, operator-facing |

Always know which target you are writing for. Never blend them.

## 2. Neil Personal — Non-Negotiable Rules

### Opening line
Neil opens with one of four patterns — no warmup, no preamble:

**A. Operator quote:**
Begin with a verbatim quote from a client or prospect conversation.
> "We already have preferred pricing." I hear this in many conversations with independent restaurant owners. My follow-up is always the same: preferred compared to what?

**B. Case study setup:**
Open inside a specific client situation — named amount, context, problem.
> A few months ago, we started working with a growing multi-unit group doing about $6M in annual food spend.

**C. Common behavior observation:**
Name something many operators do, then reveal the catch.
> Many of the restaurant operators we meet with shop their SKUs between multiple distributors each week.

**D. Direct challenge:**
State something most operators have never seen or don't know.
> Most independent restaurant operators have never seen a national chain-level distributor contract.

Never open with:
- "I wanted to share..."
- "Excited to announce..."
- A question to the reader
- A bullet list from line 1

### Body
- Full sentences, formal grammar — no fragments for emphasis
- No ellipses (those are the paid-ad voice)
- Paragraphs of 1–3 sentences each; 4–7 paragraphs total
- Builds an argument: here's the situation → here's why it happens → here's what FSIQ does
- One short dash-list per post at most (for contract components, data gaps, etc.)
- No emojis
- No hashtags (Neil's personal posts have none)
- Length: 150–350 words

### Close
Always ends with a soft CTA:
- "DM me for a no-cost audit."
- "Feel free to DM me."
- "You can check us out at www.FoodServiceIQ.com."

Never a hard sell. Never urgency.

## 3. FSIQ Company Page — Non-Negotiable Rules

### Post types
Use one of five formats:

**Case study result** (highest engagement — use for client wins):
```
[Client name + credibility detail], partnered with FoodServiceIQ to [problem].
[1 sentence on what the situation was before.]
After partnering with FoodServiceIQ, the focus shifted to:
 ✅ [process step]
 ✅ [process step]
 ✅ [process step]
The outcome:
 → [specific dollar amount or percentage]
 → [second outcome]
 → Zero operational disruption
[Soft CTA: Download the playbook in the first comment — or message Neil Chand...]
```

**Chain vs independent insight** (use for educational posts):
Open with a counterintuitive observation about how national chains operate.
No checklist format — prose paragraphs like Neil's personal voice.
End with playbook CTA.

**Playbook promotion** (use for direct lead gen):
One-line hook → proof point (2,000 restaurants, 20+ years) → what's in the playbook → download CTA.

**Client celebration** (use for client milestones, awards, press features):
Warm, specific, human. Lead with what the client did. End with a brief mention of the partnership.
Can use one emoji (🎉 👏) for celebration posts only.

**Performance data / industry news** (use for timely commentary):
Open with the news or stat. Explain the implication for independents. End with FSIQ relevance.

### Formatting
- Uses ✅ for process steps, → for outcomes (case study posts only)
- No emojis except celebration posts (one max)
- Hashtags: 4–5 from the core set below (skip for pure thought leadership posts)
- Core hashtag set: `#IndependentRestaurants #RestaurantManagement #FoodService #SupplyChain #ProcurementStrategy`
- CTA links go in first comment, not post body (LinkedIn algorithm penalizes external links in posts)

## 4. Shared Rules — Both Targets

**No adversarial distributor framing.** The gap is structural, not personal. Distributors are not villains.

**No fear-mongering.** No "while you wait, competitors are pulling ahead." No urgency language.

**No downstream outcomes.** Never claim what clients did with their savings (new locations, staff hires, bonuses). FSIQ's claim is food cost reduction — full stop.

**No math out loud.** State results, not arithmetic. "$520,000 in savings" — not "a 5% reduction on $10M in annual food spend."

**No promotional vocabulary.** No "groundbreaking," "transformational," "innovative," "cutting-edge," "best-in-class," "exciting."

**Specific > vague.** Named clients with specific dollar amounts outperform generic claims. "Black's BBQ saved $427,000" beats "our clients save significantly on food costs."

**No "It's not X, it's Y" frames.** Cut to the positive directly.

## 5. Core Proof Points (use exactly as written)

- "$2B+ in aggregated purchasing power"
- "former Sysco COO and PFG President" (referring to Michael Wiedower)
- "2,000+ independent restaurants"
- "5–7% average reduction in annual food costs"
- "no changes to suppliers, ingredients, or SKUs"
- "100% performance-based — zero upfront cost"
- "16+ years of experience"

Never calculate with these numbers. Never say "2,000 restaurants × 5% average savings."
